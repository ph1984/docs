Pulse is configurable at the action level. You don't choose a single global mode — you decide, for each individual action Pulse can take, whether it should ask for your approval first or execute on its own. This lets you move at your own pace: start cautious, build confidence, and grant autonomy where you're ready for it.

<Warning>
  Granting full autonomy means Pulse will execute actions without asking. Start with read-only observation and approval-required actions while you calibrate your baselines. Expand autonomy incrementally as you gain confidence in Pulse's pattern recognition for your business.
</Warning>

## Autonomy settings

For every action Pulse can take — sending a notification, executing a workflow, escalating an incident — you independently choose one of two modes:

- **Ask for approval** — Pulse surfaces the finding and its suggested action, but waits for a human to confirm before doing anything.
- **Act autonomously** — Pulse executes the action immediately without waiting for approval.

The recommended starting point is approval-required for everything. As you observe how Pulse behaves and validate that its suggestions are accurate for your business, you can flip individual actions to autonomous one at a time.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Pulse settings">
    Navigate to **Pulse → Settings → Autonomy** in your iTeam dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select an action">
    You'll see a list of every action Pulse is configured to take. Select the action you want to configure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the autonomy mode">
    Toggle between **Ask for approval** and **Act autonomously**. The change takes effect immediately on the next Pulse cycle.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the action log">
    After a few cycles, review the action log for that setting to confirm Pulse is behaving as expected before expanding autonomy to additional actions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Quiet hours

Quiet hours tell Pulse when not to send notifications. During a configured quiet window, non-critical alerts are held and delivered in the next morning's daily briefing instead of waking anyone up.

<Note>
  **CRITICAL alerts always override quiet hours.** If Pulse detects a serious issue — a major conversion drop, a payment failure, a fraud spike — it will notify your team immediately regardless of the time.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open notification settings">
    Navigate to **Pulse → Settings → Notifications → Quiet Hours**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your quiet window">
    Define the start and end time for your quiet period (for example, 11 PM – 7 AM). Pulse will suppress CALM, BOOST, and ALERT notifications during this window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and confirm">
    Your quiet hours take effect immediately. You can set different windows per team member or per team channel.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Set quiet hours per individual, not just per team. An on-call engineer may want full notifications overnight while the rest of the team does not.
</Tip>

## Monitoring interval

Pulse manages its monitoring interval automatically based on what it observes:

| State | Interval |
|---|---|
| Calm — all metrics within normal range | Every 10 minutes |
| Anomaly detected — elevated or critical state | Every 2 minutes |

You don't need to configure this. Pulse handles the acceleration and deceleration automatically, returning to the 10-minute cadence once a situation is resolved.

If your infrastructure requires a minimum floor on query frequency (for example, to protect a database under heavy load), you can set a **minimum interval floor** in **Pulse → Settings → Monitoring → Interval Floor**. Setting a floor will prevent Pulse from accelerating below that threshold even during anomalies.

## Connecting data sources

Pulse needs access to your business data to run its analysis cycles. Supported source types include:

- **Databases** — PostgreSQL, MySQL, and other SQL-compatible databases
- **CRM platforms** — connect your customer records and activity data
- **Analytics platforms** — link to your existing product or web analytics
- **Custom APIs** — connect any internal or third-party data source via REST API

To add or manage data source connections, navigate to **Pulse → Settings → Data Sources**, or see the full connection guide in [Integrations](/integrations/overview).

<Note>
  Pulse connects to your data sources in **read-only mode**. It will never write to, modify, or delete records in any connected source.
</Note>
