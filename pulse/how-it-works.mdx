Pulse isn't a passive dashboard alert. It runs a continuous intelligence cycle: it observes your data, interprets what changes mean in context, communicates findings in plain language, and feeds every insight back into the next analysis. Each cycle makes the next one smarter.

## The Pulse cycle

<Steps>
  <Step title="Observe">
    Every few minutes, Pulse queries your connected data sources — funnel metrics, user activity, financial data, and campaign performance. As it runs, it builds a precise baseline of what "normal" looks like for your specific business, including patterns by time of day, day of week, and seasonal variation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Interpret">
    Pulse doesn't just detect that a number changed — it understands whether that change matters. A conversion dip at 3 AM on a Sunday carries a different weight than the same dip on a Tuesday afternoon. Pulse factors in time of day, historical patterns, and recent trend direction before deciding how to classify what it sees.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Communicate">
    When something requires your attention, Pulse sends a clear, actionable message in plain executive language. No dashboards to dig through, no jargon to decode. Example: *"Conversion dropped 30% in the last hour. Pattern suggests payment gateway failure."* You get the finding and the diagnosis in one sentence.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Learn">
    Every completed cycle feeds the next one. Pulse logs its insights, compares the current analysis to previous ones, and tracks emerging trends across days and weeks. Its understanding of your business deepens continuously — baselines tighten, pattern recognition improves, and false positives decrease over time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adapt">
    Pulse automatically adjusts its monitoring interval based on what it finds. During calm periods it checks every 10 minutes, conserving resources and avoiding noise. The moment an anomaly is detected, it accelerates to every 2 minutes, maintaining close watch until the situation is understood and resolved.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Specialized Pulse agents

Pulse deploys purpose-built agents, each focused on a specific layer of your business. You can run one or several in parallel depending on what you need to monitor.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Funnel Agent" icon="filter">
    Monitors the full acquisition and activation pipeline — from signup through first deposit to recurring activity. Flags drop-offs and conversion anomalies at each stage.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Retention Agent" icon="users">
    Detects churn patterns before customers leave. Tracks engagement signals, identifies at-risk cohorts, and surfaces retention opportunities while there's still time to act.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Fraud Agent" icon="shield-halved">
    Identifies anomalous behavior in real time — unusual transaction patterns, suspicious signup clusters, and activity that deviates from established user baselines.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Performance Agent" icon="chart-line">
    Tracks campaign performance and ROI by channel. Surfaces which campaigns are over- or under-delivering and flags moments where spend reallocation would have the most impact.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  **Pulse is read-only.** It queries your data sources to observe and analyze, but it never writes to, modifies, or deletes any of your data. Your records stay exactly as they are — Pulse only reads them.
</Note>
