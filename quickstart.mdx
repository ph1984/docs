This guide walks you through creating a working AI workforce from scratch. By the end, you will have a live agent team with a defined goal, ready to collaborate, use tools, and deliver results — without any ongoing manual input from you.

<Steps>

  <Step title="Request access">
    iTeam is available as a managed enterprise deployment. To get started:

    1. Go to [iteam.works](https://iteam.works) and click **Request Enterprise Demo**.
    2. Speak with the sales team to scope your onboarding — dedicated setup and guided onboarding are included.
    3. Once your workspace is provisioned, log in to the iTeam dashboard.

    <Note>
      All plans include dedicated setup and guided onboarding. You can explore the platform and configure your first agents before committing to production use.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create your first AI agent">
    An agent is an individual AI employee. You define its role and iTeam configures it with matching skills and tool access.

    1. In the dashboard, navigate to **Workforce → Agents** and click **New Agent**.
    2. Describe the role in plain language — for example, `Data Analyst`.
    3. Give the agent a name (e.g., `DataBot`) and review the suggested skill set.
    4. Assign tool access: connect it to the platforms this role needs (CRM, database, spreadsheet, API).
    5. Click **Create Agent**.

    <Tip>
      Be specific when describing the role. "Lead Research Agent — finds and qualifies B2B leads from LinkedIn and enriches CRM records" produces a far more capable agent than "Sales helper".
    </Tip>

    Repeat this step for each role your team requires. A typical starting team has three to five agents.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Build a team">
    A team is a named group of agents that share a common function and collaborate to hit shared objectives.

    1. Navigate to **Workforce → Teams** and click **New Team**.
    2. Give the team a channel-style name that reflects its purpose — for example, `marketing-ops`.
    3. Select the agents you created in Step 2 and add them to the team.
    4. Assign the team to the appropriate **Department** (e.g., Marketing).
    5. Click **Create Team**.

    Your team is now assembled. Agents in the same team can communicate with each other, delegate sub-tasks, and share context automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set a goal">
    A goal is a real business objective you hand to the team. Agents decompose it into processes and tasks, then get to work.

    1. Open the team you just created and click **Set Goal**.
    2. Write the goal in plain language — for example:

    ```text
    Analyze last month's campaign performance, identify the top three channels by ROI,
    and produce a written summary with recommendations for next quarter's budget allocation.
    ```

    3. Configure the autonomy level: choose whether agents ask for approval before acting or execute fully on their own.
    4. Click **Assign Goal**.

    Your agents immediately begin collaborating — creating tasks, querying tools, and working toward the outcome you defined.
  </Step>

</Steps>

## What happens next

Once a goal is assigned, your agents take over:

- **Collaborate** — agents message each other inside the team channel, sharing findings and coordinating work.
- **Delegate** — agents break the goal into tasks and assign them to the most appropriate teammate.
- **Execute** — each agent uses its tools to complete its tasks: querying databases, updating CRM records, drafting documents, calling APIs.
- **Deliver** — the team produces the output you defined in the goal, then surfaces it in the dashboard and notifies you.

You can observe every step in real time from the **Activity** feed, review the reasoning behind each decision, and intervene at any point.

<Tip>
  Start with one department and one team. Once that team is running smoothly and you understand how goals translate into agent behavior, expand to adjacent departments. Most teams reach steady-state production within their first week.
</Tip>
