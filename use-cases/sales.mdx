Your sales reps are most valuable when they're in front of prospects — not hunting for contact data, drafting cold emails, or logging call notes in the CRM. iTeam gives your team a force multiplier: AI agents that handle the research, draft the outreach, and keep records current, so every rep walks into every conversation prepared and focused on closing.

## The sales team setup

Three agents cover the end-to-end prospecting and pipeline management workflow. Each one specializes in a distinct function, and together they eliminate the manual overhead that eats into selling time.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Lead Research Agent" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Finds and qualifies leads against your ICP criteria, enriches contact and company data from connected sources, and scores each prospect so your reps know exactly who to prioritize and why.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Outreach Agent" icon="envelope">
    Takes the research brief and drafts personalized outreach emails and follow-up sequences tailored to each prospect's role, company context, and pain points. No generic templates — every message reflects actual research.
  </Card>
  <Card title="CRM Agent" icon="database">
    Logs every interaction automatically — emails sent, calls completed, meetings booked. Updates pipeline stages, records contact data changes, and keeps your CRM accurate in real time so the team always has a clean view of the pipeline.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## A typical prospecting cycle

From ICP definition to a first meeting on the calendar, here's how the three agents operate together.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define your ICP">
    Give the **Lead Research Agent** your ideal customer profile — industry, company size, title, tech stack, geography, or whatever criteria matter to your business. This becomes the filter for every prospect it surfaces.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Research and qualify">
    The Lead Research Agent works through your lead sources, enriches contact data, and scores prospects against your ICP. It produces a prioritized list with a research brief for each contact.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Draft outreach">
    The **Outreach Agent** picks up the research briefs and drafts a personalized first-touch email for each prospect. It uses the context — company news, role-specific pain points, mutual connections — to write something that doesn't read like a mass blast.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sequence and follow up">
    Once the first email is approved and sent, the Outreach Agent manages the follow-up sequence. It tracks responses, adjusts timing, and pauses sequences automatically when a prospect replies.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log and update">
    The **CRM Agent** records every action: email sent, reply received, meeting booked. It updates the prospect's pipeline stage and ensures the contact record reflects the latest activity — without any manual input from the rep.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Hand off to rep">
    When a meeting is booked, the CRM Agent notifies the rep and attaches the full research brief to the calendar event. The rep walks in knowing the prospect's background, company context, and the thread of conversation so far.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Connecting your CRM

The CRM Agent is only as useful as the data it can access and update. Connect it directly to your CRM so it has the permissions it needs to do its job.

<Note>
  The CRM Agent requires **read-write access** to your CRM to log interactions and update pipeline stages. Read-only access will let it surface information to reps but won't allow it to keep records current. See the [CRM & ERP integrations](/integrations/crm-erp) guide for supported platforms and connection steps.
</Note>

Once connected, configure which fields the CRM Agent should update automatically (e.g., last contact date, pipeline stage, next action) versus which fields require rep review before saving. Start conservative — you can loosen these rules as you build confidence in the agent's accuracy.

## Setting up your sales team

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the three agents">
    In iTeam, create the Lead Research Agent, Outreach Agent, and CRM Agent. Group them into a Sales team so they can share context and delegate tasks between each other.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define your ICP as process knowledge">
    Document your ideal customer profile and add it as process knowledge on the Lead Research Agent. Include firmographic criteria, buying signals, disqualifiers, and any nuance that distinguishes a good lead from a bad one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your lead sources">
    Give the Lead Research Agent access to your prospecting tools — LinkedIn, Apollo, your own database, or whatever sources you use. The richer the input, the more complete the research briefs it produces.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure outreach templates and tone">
    Add your messaging framework, approved value propositions, and tone guidelines as process knowledge on the Outreach Agent. This guides the personalization without letting the agent go off-brand.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your CRM">
    Integrate the CRM Agent with your CRM platform. Set field-update permissions and configure which pipeline stage transitions should trigger notifications to reps.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run a pilot prospecting cycle">
    Start with a small batch — 20 to 50 prospects — and review the outputs before enabling full automation. Check research quality, outreach tone, and CRM updates. Adjust process knowledge based on what you find.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use the Lead Research Agent's brief as a pre-call prep document for every discovery meeting. Hand it to the rep 30 minutes before the call. It typically saves 20–30 minutes of manual research per meeting — and reps consistently report going in better prepared than before.
</Tip>
