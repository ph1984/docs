Marketing teams are pulled in every direction at once: researching audiences, producing content across channels, monitoring campaign performance, and doing it all fast enough to stay relevant. iTeam lets you build a coordinated AI marketing team that handles this entire loop — so your marketers spend their time on strategy and creative direction, not on the execution grind.

## The marketing team setup

Three agents cover the research-to-results workflow, each feeding the next in a continuous cycle that gets smarter with every campaign.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Research Agent" icon="chart-bar">
    Analyzes audience data, tracks competitor activity, identifies trending topics, and reviews campaign performance to generate structured briefs that drive every content decision. It turns raw data into direction.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Content Agent" icon="pen-nib">
    Takes the research brief and produces drafts: blog posts, email copy, social content, ad variations, landing page text. Every draft is grounded in what the Research Agent found — not guesswork.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Performance Agent" icon="gauge-high">
    Monitors live campaign metrics across channels, flags underperforming assets before they drain budget, and surfaces specific optimization opportunities — which ad to pause, which email to resend, which headline to test next.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## A content production cycle

The three agents form a self-reinforcing loop. Each campaign informs the next brief, and each brief produces better content.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Research Agent generates a brief">
    The **Research Agent** pulls together audience insights, competitor positioning, trending topics in your space, and performance data from previous campaigns. It synthesizes this into a structured brief: audience, angle, key messages, and recommended format.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Content Agent produces drafts">
    The **Content Agent** receives the brief and produces first drafts across the formats you've requested — a blog post, three email subject line options, five social variations, or whatever the campaign calls for. Your team reviews, edits, and approves before anything goes live.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Content goes live">
    Approved content is published or scheduled through your connected marketing tools. The Performance Agent begins monitoring from the moment each asset is live.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Performance Agent tracks results">
    The **Performance Agent** watches click-through rates, conversion rates, engagement, and cost metrics in real time. When something underperforms, it flags it with context — not just "CTR is low" but "CTR is 40% below benchmark for this segment, consider testing a different CTA."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Research Agent updates the next brief">
    At the end of each cycle, the Performance Agent's findings feed back to the **Research Agent**. It incorporates what worked and what didn't into the next brief, so each campaign iteration starts from a stronger position.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Pulse for marketing

<Note>
  The Performance Agent works well alongside **iTeam Pulse**. Pulse is designed for continuous anomaly detection — it watches your marketing metrics around the clock and alerts you the moment something unusual happens, like a sudden conversion drop or an unexpected traffic spike. The Performance Agent then provides the deeper, marketing-specific analysis: which assets are involved, what the likely causes are, and what to do about it. Use both together for the fastest detection-to-action loop.
</Note>

## Setting up your marketing team

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the three agents">
    In iTeam, create the Research Agent, Content Agent, and Performance Agent. Group them into a Marketing team so they share context and can pass briefs and findings between each other automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your data sources">
    Give the Research Agent access to your analytics platform, social listening tools, and any audience data you have. The more it can read, the sharper the briefs it produces.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your marketing tools">
    Link the Content Agent to your content management system or publishing tools so approved drafts can move directly into production without manual copy-paste. Connect the Performance Agent to your ad platforms, email platform, and analytics dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Load your brand guidelines">
    Add your brand voice guide, tone of voice documentation, style rules, and any formatting preferences as process knowledge on the Content Agent. This is the most important configuration step for content quality.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set performance benchmarks">
    Give the Performance Agent your baseline metrics — target CTR, conversion rate, CPC, or whatever KPIs you track. Without benchmarks, it can't flag deviations. Review and update these as your baselines shift.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run a pilot campaign cycle">
    Commission a brief on a topic you're already planning to cover. Review the Research Agent's output, the Content Agent's draft, and the Performance Agent's monitoring setup. Adjust process knowledge based on the quality of each output before scaling to full campaign volume.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  The single highest-impact configuration for the Content Agent is a detailed, specific brand voice document. Don't just say "professional and friendly" — include writing examples, things to avoid, preferred vocabulary, sentence length guidance, and how tone shifts by channel (blog vs. email vs. social). The more specific you are, the less editing your team has to do on every draft.
</Tip>
