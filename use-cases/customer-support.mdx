Your customers expect fast, accurate answers at any hour. iTeam lets you deploy a coordinated team of AI agents that handle the full ticket lifecycle — from first contact to resolution — so your human team stops drowning in repetitive L1 work and starts focusing on the cases that actually need them.

## The support team setup

Three agents work together to cover every stage of a support interaction. You configure each one with your helpdesk integration, knowledge base, and escalation rules — then let them run.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="L1 Support Agent" icon="headset">
    Reads every incoming ticket, classifies urgency, and immediately resolves common issues using your approved response templates. It's the first line of contact — always available, never backlogged.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Knowledge Agent" icon="book-open">
    When a ticket needs more than a canned response, this agent searches your knowledge base and previous resolution history to surface the right answer. It feeds findings to the L1 or Resolution Agent to keep things moving.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Resolution Agent" icon="circle-check">
    Takes ownership of complex or escalated cases. It investigates, drafts detailed responses, decides when a human needs to step in, closes tickets, and logs every outcome so your knowledge base keeps improving.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How a ticket gets resolved

This is the collaboration chain in action — from the moment a ticket lands to the moment it closes.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Ticket arrives">
    A new ticket hits your helpdesk. The **L1 Support Agent** picks it up immediately, reads the content, and classifies it by urgency, category, and customer tier.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Immediate resolution attempt">
    The L1 Support Agent checks if the issue matches a known resolution pattern. If it does, it responds directly and closes the ticket — no other agents needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Knowledge lookup">
    For anything outside the common patterns, the L1 Agent hands off to the **Knowledge Agent**, which searches your documentation, FAQs, and historical tickets to find a relevant answer or precedent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complex case handling">
    If the Knowledge Agent's findings aren't enough to resolve the issue, the **Resolution Agent** takes over. It drafts a detailed response, coordinates with the relevant tools (billing systems, account records, product data), and sends the reply.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Escalation or closure">
    The Resolution Agent decides: can this be closed, or does a human need to review? If escalation is needed, it routes the ticket with full context already written up. Either way, it logs the outcome and updates the knowledge base.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What your team focuses on

Once the three-agent team is handling your L1 queue autonomously, your human support staff redirects their energy to work that actually requires human judgment.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Complex cases">
    Your team handles escalations that the Resolution Agent flags — edge cases, high-value accounts, emotionally sensitive situations, and issues that require product or engineering input.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Knowledge base quality">
    Use the resolution logs the agents produce to spot gaps in your documentation. Every ticket the agents struggle with is a signal about what to improve, add, or clarify.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Process improvement">
    Review agent performance weekly — response accuracy, escalation rate, resolution time. Adjust escalation rules and process knowledge to keep the system getting sharper.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Setting up your support team

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the three agents">
    In iTeam, create the L1 Support Agent, Knowledge Agent, and Resolution Agent. Assign each one a clear role description and connect them as a team so they can collaborate and delegate tasks to each other.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your helpdesk">
    Integrate iTeam with your ticketing system (Zendesk, Intercom, Freshdesk, or similar). The L1 Support Agent needs read and write access to pick up new tickets and post responses.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Load your knowledge base">
    Give the Knowledge Agent access to your documentation, FAQ library, and historical ticket archive. The richer this source, the more accurately it can find the right answer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set escalation rules">
    Define when the Resolution Agent should hand off to a human — by ticket category, customer tier, sentiment score, or number of failed resolution attempts. Configure these as process knowledge on the Resolution Agent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure response tone">
    Add your brand voice guidelines and response tone as process knowledge on the L1 Support Agent and Resolution Agent. This keeps every AI-generated reply consistent with how your human team writes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable autonomous responses">
    Once you're satisfied with agent behavior in observation mode, switch the team to autonomous. Agents will now respond, escalate, and close tickets without needing approval for each action.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Start with read-only observation mode for at least one week before enabling autonomous responses. During that window, review every draft reply the agents would have sent, flag inaccuracies, and use those findings to calibrate the Knowledge Agent on your actual FAQs. This single step dramatically reduces early errors and builds team confidence in the system.
</Tip>
